How to evaluate ESG performance?

 Model Description

We use a rule-based scoring model : specific scoring rules, weights, and thresholds are set to extract structured data and convert it into a composite score and rating to evaluate the ESG performance of a particular company.

 Scoring Methodology

To assess and quantify a company's performance in terms of environmental, social responsibility, and governance (ESG) factors. These scores assist investors, companies, and stakeholders in understanding a company’s sustainability and social responsibility risks. This, in turn, supports sustainable business practices, helping governments, businesses, and investors identify and manage non-financial risks, thereby promoting sustainable development.

The key trends and patterns in ESG performance

 ESG Trends in the Healthcare Sector

The Environmental (E) and Governance (G) scores for the healthcare sector show a generally stable or upward trend from 2020 to 2022, indicating ongoing improvement in these areas. In contrast, the Social (S) score remains low and fluctuates slightly, suggesting ongoing challenges in social responsibility initiatives.

From 2020 to 2022, the healthcare sector has shown improvements in Waste and GHG Emissions management and Supply Chain Management, indicating stronger environmental practices and supply chain efficiency. However, areas like Systemic Risk and Data Security have declined, highlighting potential weaknesses in managing risks and securing data effectively.

 Strengths and Weaknesses in ESG Practices

The healthcare sector excels in environmental management, including water and hazardous materials practices, and demonstrates a strong commitment to employee engagement and diversity. However, weaknesses in customer welfare, privacy, and business model resilience highlight areas where the sector could improve to enhance overall ESG performance.
