ESG concept

Environmental, social and governance (ESG) criteria are a set of standards for a company’s operations that socially conscious investors use to screen potential investments.

  • Environmental criteria consider how a company performs as a steward of nature.
  • Social criteria examine how it manages relationships with employees, suppliers, customers, and the communities where it operates.
  • Governance deals with a company’s ​leadership, executive pay, audits, internal controls, and shareholder rights.

Environmental
At the heart of the environment are issues related to climate change and carbon emissions.
For the sustainability and survival of human beings all over the world, enterprises must resolutely reduce carbon emissions in the future and go further to pursue carbon zero. At the same time, resource and waste management to alleviate environmental pollution, as well as energy efficiency that consumes less energy and resources, have also become important issues.

Social
On the social front, companies should work to guarantee human rights, protect data, consider diversity, and build partnerships with supply chains and communities.

Governance
In terms of governance structure, transparent and credible board composition and support enterprises to achieve such environmental and social values
An audit committee needs to be established. In addition, high governance values can be ensured by observing corporate ethics in lobbying and political donation activities by preventing bribery or corruption.
